Description

The FF-70 is an autofocus compact camera for 35mm film, introduced by Ricoh in 1985 as the successor to their FF-3 AF. This camera is also known as the FF-90 in North America. In Japan, it was widely marketed with the name of Myport LUI.

Tech Specs

Lens: Rikenon Lens 35mm f/2.8, 5 elements in 5 groups.
Focus: Single-point active autofocusing system, 10 focusing zones, Pre-focus facility.
Shutter: Programmed, 2 sec. - 1/500 sec.
Flash: Built-in flash.
Self-timer.
Film transport: Auto-loading, advancing and rewinding.
Battery: 2 x AA.
